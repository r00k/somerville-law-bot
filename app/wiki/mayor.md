Somerville's mayor is the city's chief executive, elected at large to a full-time, two-year term that begins the first business day in January after the municipal election (Charter Sec. 3-1). Any registered voter is eligible to hold the office (Sec. 3-1(c)). The mayor may not hold another paid city position or elected office, and for one year after leaving cannot take a paid appointed city job or receive pay for work contracted during their tenure (Sec. 3-2). Salary is set by city council ordinance, but changes require 8 council votes, must be adopted before the last 6 months of the term, and take effect only after the next election (Sec. 3-3).

All executive power is vested solely in the mayor, who runs city agencies personally or through them and signs deeds, leases, and contracts for the city (Sec. 3-4; also Sec. 2-21). The mayor sits on every appointed multiple-member body by virtue of office but cannot vote on those bodies (Sec. 3-4(c)). Major appointments — department heads, the city attorney (2-year term), the chief administrative officer, and members of many boards — generally require city council confirmation (Secs. 3-5, 3-6, 2-24). Temporary appointments to vacancies are capped (180 days for offices, 150 for boards) with council-approved extensions (Secs. 3-7, 3-8). The mayor also files an annual budget with a five-year capital improvements plan (Sec. 2-23).

Ordinances passed by council go to the mayor, who may sign or veto them; a veto can be overridden by 8 council votes, and unsigned measures become law after 10 days (Sec. 3-9). See related topics on the city council and veto override for details.

If the mayor is temporarily unable to serve, the council president (or an elected councilor) becomes acting mayor with limited powers (Sec. 3-11). For a permanent vacancy, the office is filled at the next election if it's within 6 months, otherwise by a special election within 150 days, with the council president serving as acting mayor meanwhile (Sec. 3-13).

Note: The charter does not appear to set any term limit on how many times a mayor may be re-elected.
