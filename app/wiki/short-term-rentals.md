Somerville regulates short-term rentals — renting a residential unit for fewer than 28 consecutive days, whether or not through a booking agent like Airbnb or VRBO (Sec. 7-251). The rules took effect January 1, 2020, with registration required starting April 1, 2023 (Secs. 7-259, 7-254(j)).

Key rules (Sec. 7-253): you must have a certificate of registration from Inspectional Services (ISD) before operating; the unit must be your **primary residence** (where you live at least 9 of 12 months); you may rent to only one party at a time (no separate rooms to separate parties); guests are capped at 10, or two per bedroom, whichever is fewer; only one listing per booking agent; and no hourly rentals, stays under 10 hours, or commercial meetings. Your registration number must appear in every listing, and you must post a sign inside with trash, parking, noise, and emergency-contact information (Sec. 7-253(n),(o)).

An important distinction: a **shared unit** (you stay on-site, one bedroom reserved for you) can be rented 365 days a year, but an **autonomous unit** (whole unit, you're not there) is limited to 90 days per year total (Sec. 7-253(g),(h)).

To register (Sec. 7-254): apply online, pay a **$250 fee**, and submit proof of primary residence, owner approval if you're a tenant, a Massachusetts DOR registration certificate, and proof of insurance. ISD inspects before issuing. Certificates **expire each March 31** and are not automatically transferable if the unit is sold or leased.

Gotchas: units with outstanding building, sanitary, zoning, or fire violations can't be registered, and existing registrations are suspended if a violation arises (Sec. 7-253(j)). Three or more violations in six months make the property ineligible for six months (Sec. 7-253(k)). Violations carry noncriminal penalties per Sec. 1-11, with each day a separate offense (Sec. 7-256).

Note that "residential unit" excludes hotels, motels, group residences, and similar (Sec. 7-251). A Bed & Breakfast is a separate zoning use category (§ 9.2.9) requiring a special permit in a Local Historic District — see zoning topics for that.
