Somerville holds **non-partisan** municipal elections — ballots carry no party marks or political designations (Sec. 7-5; older Sec. 9). The **regular municipal election** is held on the Tuesday after the first Monday in November in odd-numbered years (Sec. 7-3), electing the mayor, at-large and ward councilors, and school committee members. A **preliminary election** to narrow the field is held on the second Tuesday in September of those years, though the city clerk (with council approval) may move it to the third Tuesday to avoid a holiday conflict (Sec. 7-1).

To get on the ballot, a candidate files nomination forms with certified voter signatures. Under the current charter (Sec. 7-2), the minimums are: mayor 250, at-large councilor 100, ward councilor 50 (all from that ward), and school committee member 50 (all from that ward). Forms are available April 2–June 18 and must reach the city clerk by 5:00 p.m. on the 45th day before the preliminary election. Note: the older Charter Appendix (Sec. 3) lists different figures (250/150/100); the current charter numbers govern. A person may appear on the ballot for only one office (Sec. 7-2).

If no more than the allowed number of candidates file (2 for mayor/ward/school committee, 8 for at-large), they are deemed nominated and **no preliminary is held** for that office (Sec. 7-4/7-2(e); older Sec. 14). Ballot order is set by a public drawing by the city clerk (Sec. 7-2(c), 7-4). Ties for the last ballot position put all tied candidates on the November ballot (Sec. 7-2(d)(3)).

**Voter registration:** forms are available at all branch libraries, the traffic and parking department lobby, and City Hall lobby (Sec. 2-8). The **Board of Election Commissioners** (four members, split between the two leading parties) oversees election conduct and voter registers (Sec. 2, 5, 7 of Article IV). The city is divided into 7 wards (Sec. 7-6).

Anything not covered here — voter eligibility, absentee/early voting, recounts, polling-place rules — is governed by Massachusetts General Laws, not these local sections (Sec. 7-7).
