Somerville treats two different kinds of dumpsters under separate rules. **Temporary construction dumpsters** (the roll-off type used during building or renovation work) are covered by Sec. 12-77: it is unlawful to place one on any public or private street without first getting a permit from the superintendent of inspectional services. Violating any permit condition is itself a violation, penalties follow Sec. 1-11(b), and each day counts as a separate offense. These construction dumpsters are expressly excluded from the Board of Health trash regulations (see the definition in Section 1) and are instead handled by the Inspectional Services Building Division or the Fire Department depending on the work and location.

**Standing trash/recycling dumpsters** (any receptacle 75 gallons or larger for household or commercial waste, plus all commercial grease containers) fall under the Board of Health "Regulations for the Removal or Transportation of Trash." Since July 1, 1998, no such dumpster may be used or kept without a license from the Board (Section 2.1), applied for through Inspectional Services Health Division; licenses expire each June 30 and cost $100 per dumpster (Sections 2.3–2.4). Companies that service or haul dumpsters need a separate Dumpster Hauler License, $330/year (Section 3).

Operating rules in Section 4 are detailed: tight-fitting covers kept closed and locked 11 p.m.–7 a.m. (4.1); no emptying or removal 7 p.m.–7 a.m. (4.2); labels showing the business and hauler (4.3); placement away from lot lines, off soil onto pavement, not blocking traffic sightlines, and in compliance with zoning (4.4, 4.6, 4.14); daily emptying of liquids/organics (4.7); and being kept free of pests and overflow (4.8–4.9). Placing a dumpster on a city street also requires Public Works permission (4.11), and food-service sites must meet the State Sanitary Code (4.12).

Common gotchas: violations must be corrected within 12 hours of notice (6.1), fines run $50/day per offense with each day a separate violation (6.4–6.5), the Board can suspend or revoke licenses (6.3), and inspections are unannounced with a right of entry (6.8). Transporting collected trash through city streets requires additional Board of Health licensing or registration (Sections 7–8). These regulations apply to residential, commercial, and industrial dumpsters alike (6.6). For trash pickup generally, see related solid-waste topics.
