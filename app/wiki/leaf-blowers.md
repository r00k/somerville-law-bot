Somerville regulates when and how leaf blowers can be used under Sec. 9-120. A "leaf blower" is any portable, handheld or backpack power blower—gas, battery, or electric—used to move leaves, dirt, grass clippings, debris, and similar material (Sec. 9-120(a)). The rules also define "owners of large property" (OLP), meaning adjoining parcels totaling two acres or more, and "commercial leaf blower operator," meaning an entity employing two or more paid operators (municipal operators and contractors are excluded).

Key rules under Sec. 9-120(b):
- **Seasons:** Use is prohibited except March 15–May 31 and October 1–December 15 (subsection (b)(1)).
- **Hours:** Prohibited on Sundays and legal holidays, and only allowed 9:00 a.m.–5:00 p.m. Monday through Saturday (subsection (b)(2)).
- **No blowing onto others' property:** Debris may not be blown onto adjacent parcels, streets, gutters, catch basins, vehicles, people, or pets, and must be cleaned up and disposed of properly (subsection (b)(4)).
- **Noise/emissions:** All blowers must meet EPA emissions standards and be rated by the manufacturer at no more than 65 decibels (subsection (b)(5)).
- **Number in use:** On lots of 10,000 sq. ft. or less, only one blower at a time; larger lots allow one per 10,000 sq. ft. area (subsection (b)(6)).

Common gotchas and exceptions: A resident using a blower on their own home for a "de minimus" amount—no more than five minutes per day total—is exempt from the seasonal and hour limits, but still must follow the other noise ordinance rules in Secs. 9-112 to 9-119 (subsection (b)(7)). Commercial operators and OLP cannot use blowers unless they submit an operations plan to the Director of Inspectional Services for approval, and even then must still follow the season and hour limits (subsection (b)(3)). Certain city parks, fields, and emergency/storm cleanup by municipal operators are exempt from the season and hour rules. The city may charge testing and enforcement fees (subsection (c)), and Inspectional Services may issue regulations (subsection (d)). The ordinance took effect September 1, 2021, with a phase-in for the decibel limit on city-owned equipment (subsection (f)).

The provided text does not specify the penalty or fine amount for violations, so check with Inspectional Services on enforcement. See related noise rules in Secs. 9-112 to 9-119.
