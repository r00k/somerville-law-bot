Somerville's noise rules live mainly in Chapter 9, Article VII (Secs. 9-112 through 9-119). Sec. 9-112 broadly makes it unlawful to make any "loud, unnecessary or unusual noise" that annoys, disturbs, or endangers the comfort, health, peace, or safety of others. The ordinance targets preventable noise, not "the usual and customary noise incidental to urban life" (Sec. 9-113).

Sec. 9-116 lists many specific prohibited "noise disturbances," including barking animals, loud air conditioners, engine exhaust without a muffler, unnecessary horn honking, and construction noise. Key time limits to know:

- **Construction (Sec. 9-116(3)):** generally only allowed 7:00 a.m.–7:00 p.m. weekdays and 9:00 a.m.–7:00 p.m. Saturdays; never on Sundays or Massachusetts legal holidays. Extra-loud equipment (jackhammers, pile drivers, blasting, concrete saws, rock crushers) is barred after 5:00 p.m. weekdays and all day Saturday.
- **Domestic power tools outdoors (Sec. 9-116(4)):** banned 9:00 p.m.–7:00 a.m. weekdays, and 9:00 p.m.–9:00 a.m. weekends/holidays.
- **Radios, TVs, instruments (Sec. 9-116(13)):** no disturbing sound 11:00 p.m.–7:00 a.m.
- **Loudspeakers/PA systems (Sec. 9-116(12)):** no use 10:00 p.m.–7:00 a.m.
- **Alarms (Sec. 9-116(5)):** must shut off within minutes; car alarms sounding over ten minutes can be towed at the owner's cost.

Sec. 9-117 also sets measurable decibel limits by time of day and district (for example, 40–75 db depending on duration and hour), measured at the property line or in an adjoining apartment. Note that motor vehicles on public highways, power lawn/garden tools, and pile drivers/jackhammers/construction equipment are exempt from the decibel-table rules under Sec. 9-117(3) — but they can still be limited by the time restrictions in Sec. 9-116.

Enforcement is by the police chief and inspectional services via noncriminal citation with fines under Sec. 1-11 (Sec. 9-118), and each violation is a separate offense.

A separate rule, Sec. 9-97 (Nuisance control), targets loud or unruly gatherings/parties on private property; notably it does **not** require any scientific noise measurement to issue a citation, and repeat responses within a year can make the property owner liable. See the "Nuisance / disorderly gatherings" and "Dogs / animals" topics for related provisions (barking dogs are also addressed in Sec. 3-33). The provided sections do not specify exact fine amounts (they refer to Sec. 1-11).
