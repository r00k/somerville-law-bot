Somerville regulates bicycles, non-motorized conveyances (scooters and other human-powered wheeled devices), and motorized scooters mainly through the Traffic Commission Regulations (Appendix E, Articles XIII and XVII), plus street-design and bike-parking rules elsewhere in the code.

**Where you can ride (Sec. 13-4, 13-5, 13-6).** Bicycles may be operated in general traffic or on sidewalks, and pedestrians always have the right-of-way on sidewalks and in crosswalks (Sec. 13-4). Other human-powered conveyances (e.g., non-motorized scooters) must generally use sidewalks. Riders must dismount and walk in the business districts listed in Sec. 13-5 — including designated stretches of Davis, Union, Porter, Ball, Teele, and Magoun Squares, plus parts of Broadway. In bike lanes, bicycles have exclusive use and cars and pedestrians are barred (Sec. 13-6); when turning left, cyclists move to the center of the roadway (Sec. 13-7).

**Parking your bike (Sec. 13-8).** Bikes may be locked to public appurtenances like light poles, street signs, and parking meters, but not to shade trees, benches, or trash receptacles. Improperly secured bikes, or bikes left in place more than 15 days, may be impounded; violations carry a $15 penalty. Enforcement and penalties for moving/parking violations appear in Sec. 13-9 (e.g., $20 or impoundment for riders under 18; $50 for refusing/falsifying name).

**Motorized scooters (Article XVII)** — electric or gas-powered scooters that aren't "motor vehicles" or "motorized bicycles" (Segways and disability mobility devices are excluded, Sec. 17-2). Key rules in Sec. 17-3: minimum age 16 with a license/permit; 25 mph speed cap; helmet required; no passengers; must use bike lanes when within 10 yards; no sidewalk riding, no nighttime operation, and no streets posted over 30 mph. Fines run up to $25 (first offense) rising to $50–$100 (Sec. 17-3(o)).

**Note on e-bikes:** the provided sections do not define or set specific rules for "electric bicycles" as a distinct category, so this corpus doesn't clearly answer questions unique to e-bikes.

**Bike infrastructure.** The Safe Streets Ordinance (Sec. 12-116 et seq.) commits the city to protected/separated bike lanes under the Somerville Bicycle Network Plan, requiring at least 29 miles of separated bicycle facilities by December 31, 2030 (Sec. 12-116.3). A Bicycle Advisory Committee advises on these issues (Sec. 2-300 to 2-303), and Article VIII (Sec. 12-117 et seq.) requires side guards and mirrors on large city-contract vehicles to protect cyclists and other vulnerable road users.

**New-development bike parking.** The Zoning Ordinance § 11.1 sets detailed short-term and long-term bicycle parking standards (rack/locker design, spacing, weather protection, accessible routes) for new construction — see § 11.1.1–11.1.4.
