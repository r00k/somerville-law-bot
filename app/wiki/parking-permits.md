Somerville has city-wide resident permit parking: since 2010 all public streets fall under resident permit parking unless otherwise posted (Schedule E was retired when regulation 2009-23 took effect). Street-by-street restrictions (time limits, "2 hour except by permit," permit-only overnight hours) are listed in Schedule D.

Residents whose vehicle (except commercial vehicles) is registered and principally garaged in Somerville can apply for a residential permit under Sec. 15-2.1. You must submit the application, a current Massachusetts registration showing your Somerville address, and a current utility bill. Each qualifying resident gets one residential permit and may receive up to two visitor passes. Visitor passes must be fully visible, may be used no more than two days in any one-week period (except Sundays), and if lost may be replaced only twice per year.

Guest/visitor permits (Sec. 5-8) may only be used by actual guests of a resident on that street or an abutting street. A Somerville resident may not use a guest permit on their own vehicle — doing so triggers revocation of all guest permits in the household for up to one year. A vehicle displaying guest permits is compliant for no more than two days per week (Mon–Sat) unless the Traffic Commission grants an extension.

Big gotcha: permits are prohibited for residents of addresses that did not receive their first certificate of occupancy before January 15, 2020 (see Map A and Schedule W for specific prohibited addresses). The Director of Parking may waive this for residents with disabilities, residents of deed-restricted affordable units, or those with extenuating circumstances, with required documentation. Landlords and sellers must disclose these restrictions in ads and notify buyers/tenants in writing (Sec. 7.2).

Fees are set in Sec. 15-3 — for example, $40 residential permit, $20 for a 2-day visitor permit — with no charge for persons with disabilities or those 65+. Other permit types include business (Sec. 15-2.2, awarded by lottery), city employee (Sec. 15-2.3), citywide placards, and valet parking (Sec. 15-2.4).

Handicapped residential spaces are approved under Sec. 5-15 (see Schedule H for locations) and require a Massachusetts HP plate/placard, residency, and an engineering study; such spaces are not for the applicant's exclusive use.

If the Parking Clerk denies a permit or renewal, you may appeal to the Traffic Commission within 30 days (Sec. 16-1); its decision is final. Misuse of a permit can lead to suspension or revocation, but only after written notice and a chance to be heard (Sec. 15-6).
