Somerville addresses affordable housing through two main mechanisms: a city trust fund (Code of Ordinances Chapter 7, Article V) and zoning requirements that make new development create affordable units.

The **Somerville Municipal Affordable Housing Trust Fund** (Sec. 7-91) exists to create and preserve affordable housing for low- and moderate-income households. It is run by a 5–9 member board of trustees, including the Mayor, City Council President, and Housing Authority Director or their designees (Sec. 7-92), and can make grants and loans at its discretion (Sec. 7-93). Money paid in under zoning rules, fees, or private gifts goes directly to the trust and stays there year to year (Sec. 7-94). The trust is tax-exempt (Sec. 7-97), audited annually (Sec. 7-98), and its governing declaration is filed with the City Clerk (Sec. 7-99).

On the zoning side, an **Affordable Dwelling Unit (ADU)** is a home sold or rented at a price affordable to a specified income level (Glossary; note this is *not* a backyard "accessory dwelling unit"). Section 12.1 requires most residential development to include ADUs; these units must generally be mixed in with market-rate units, comparable in quality, and larger projects (30+ units) must include some 3-bedroom units (Sec. 12.1.7). ADU rents, sale prices, and income eligibility are set by tiers tied to Boston-area Median Family Income and published/updated annually by the Director of Housing (Sec. 12.1.5, 12.1.6). Households are chosen through a City selection system, and units must stay affordable in perpetuity via a recorded deed restriction (Sec. 12.1.6, 12.1.8).

Key exceptions and alternatives: student/faculty housing owned by educational institutions is exempt (Sec. 12.1.2); developers can sometimes pay a fractional "buyout" or an in-lieu payment (by Special Permit) instead of building units (Sec. 12.3.6, 12.3.7); and non-residential development over 15,000 sq ft pays a graduated **linkage fee** ($22.46/sq ft, inflation-adjusted) into the trust fund (Sec. 12.2.2). The **Affordable Housing (AH) overlay** (Sec. 8.1) offers extra height and density to projects where *all* units are affordable; those projects are exempt from Section 12.1.

For income eligibility as a renter or buyer, the specific dollar figures are not in the ordinance itself — they are calculated and posted on the City website by the Director of Housing, so residents seeking to qualify should check current published limits. Related topics: condominium/cooperative conversion (Article IV) uses a similar 80%-of-Area-Median-Income standard (Sec. 7-63).
