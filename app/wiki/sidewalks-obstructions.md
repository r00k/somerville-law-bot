Somerville regulates what can be placed, left, built, or done on sidewalks and streets, mainly through Chapter 12 and Chapter 11 of the Code of Ordinances.

**Leaving objects and obstructions.** You generally cannot leave vehicles, articles, or obstructions on a sidewalk or public/private way without a permit from the commissioner of public works, and permitted obstacles must be lit and guarded at night (Sec. 12-18); construction dumpsters are handled separately under Sec. 12-77. You also may not leave fuel, dirt, ashes, rubbish, or other material on a sidewalk or street during nighttime, or otherwise obstruct it, without a DPW permit (Sec. 12-12). Wood, coal, or other articles can't sit on the sidewalk for more than two hours, or more than one hour after being told to move them (Sec. 12-5). Excavations or obstructions must be guarded with railings and lights so travel stays safe (Sec. 11-84).

**Signs, awnings, outdoor seating, and sidewalk cafes.** Placing tables, stalls, booths, signs, goods, outdoor seating, or similar structures on or over a street or sidewalk requires a permit from the city council or other authorization (Sec. 12-14). The licensing commission can permit licensed establishments to place outdoor seating on the adjacent public way; those permits expire December 31 of the year granted and require a $5,000 bond or naming the city as additional insured (Sec. 12-14). Awnings must be at least seven feet high and cannot extend beyond the sidewalk line (Sec. 12-16), and posts for awnings or other purposes need city council permission (Sec. 12-17). Items placed contrary to these rules can be removed by the DPW highway superintendent (Sec. 12-19).

**Keeping sidewalks clear and clean.** Abutting owners, tenants, and occupants must remove rubbish and waste from the adjacent sidewalk daily and not leave it overnight (Sec. 11-43); businesses have similar duties (Sec. 11-44). Cellar entrances or grates in sidewalks must be kept covered and level, or railed and lit if open (Sec. 11-87).

**Vehicles, bikes, and loitering.** Driving vehicles across sidewalks is prohibited except at driveways or under a DPW permit (Sec. 12-7; Appendix E Sec. 7-20). Non-motorized conveyances (like bikes) must be walked, not ridden, on sidewalks in designated squares including Davis, Union, Porter, and others (Appendix E Sec. 13-5). Loitering or blocking foot traffic after a police order to move is prohibited (Sec. 12-11).

**Gotchas:** A "sandwich board" isn't named specifically, but signs and other articles on sidewalks fall under Sec. 12-14's permit requirement. Note the sidewalk cafe permit is annual (Sec. 12-14). The zoning ordinance also sets design standards for new/reconstructed sidewalks, including ADA accessibility and minimum widths (Zoning 13.2.6). For related rules, see topics on trash collection and street excavation.
