Somerville's ordinances broadly restrict fireworks of all kinds, including firecrackers, rockets, Roman candles, and similar devices. Under Sec. 9-88, no one may discharge, fire, or use any firecracker, rocket, torpedo, Roman candle, so-called BB gun, or other fireworks—or a pistol, cane, cannon, or other appliance using a blank cartridge or cap containing an explosive mixture—without permission from the city council. Selling or storing any fireworks is likewise prohibited without city council permission (Sec. 9-89).

Skyrockets get their own rule: Sec. 9-87 bars anyone from selling, exposing for sale, giving away, using, or discharging a sky rocket without the mayor's permission. Note the different approving authority—skyrockets go through the mayor, while other fireworks go through the city council.

Indoor pyrotechnics are separately and completely banned. Under Sec. 5-11, no person or entity may use any indoor pyrotechnic material (as defined by state regulation 527 CMR 2.03) in Somerville, and no one is even eligible to apply for a permit for an indoor pyrotechnic display. The fire and police departments enforce this through non-criminal fines under Sec. 1-11, with each violation counting as a separate offense.

A few practical notes: the ordinances don't spell out how to obtain city council or mayoral permission, or list any exceptions for items like sparklers—residents should ask the city directly. Fines and enforcement details for outdoor fireworks (Secs. 9-87 through 9-89) are not specified in these sections. For related rules, see Fire prevention and protection (Chapter 5).
