Somerville's Tree Preservation Ordinance (Chapter 12, Article VI) regulates trees on city property, private property, and in the public right-of-way (Sec. 12-101). It distinguishes three categories: **public shade trees** (in the public way, governed by state law M.G.L. c. 87), **city trees** (on parks, schoolyards, plazas, and other city land), and **private trees** (Sec. 12-102). A "significant tree" is generally a non-invasive tree 8 inches or more in diameter (DBH), or certain invasive trees 24 inches or more (Sec. 12-102).

**Removing a private tree:** You may not remove a significant tree from private property without a free tree permit from the tree warden (Sec. 12-112). Applications require a plan showing each tree; the warden must complete review within 30 business days or the permit is deemed issued (Sec. 12-112(b)). Removed significant trees must be replaced with equal-or-greater total caliper, or you can pay into the street tree stabilization fund instead (Sec. 12-112(c); Sec. 12-109). Replacements must be planted within 18 months and replaced if they die within 18 months (Sec. 12-112(d)).

**Owner-occupant break:** Owner-occupants of 1–3 family homes with a residential exemption get a 90% reduction in replanting/payment requirements without a hearing — but it's revoked if they stop living there within 18 months (Sec. 12-112(c)(5)–(6)).

**Public shade and city trees:** These cannot be removed, pruned, or damaged without the tree warden's written consent (Sec. 12-105.1); pruning requires a permit and a credentialed arborist (Sec. 12-105.2). Removing a healthy public shade tree requires a written public-welfare finding and a public hearing (Sec. 12-106, 12-110), with mailed/flyered notice to neighbors within 150 feet (Sec. 12-107). City trees get similar hearing and notice protections (Sec. 12-111), with exemptions for small trees under 1.5 inches and certain public/capital projects.

**Exceptions:** Hazardous, dead, or dying trees may be removed with the tree warden's authorization, and emergencies like storms allow waived requirements (Sec. 12-112(e)–(f); Sec. 12-111(c)(3)).

**Gotchas:** Removing a private significant tree without a permit triggers daily fines after a 30-day grace period, and a vanished tree is presumed to have been 24 inches in diameter (Sec. 12-113(c)–(d)). "Removal" includes killing a tree through poisoning or excessive pruning (Sec. 12-102). Construction near trees (within 50 feet, or 10 feet for roof/siding work) requires tree protection plans (Sec. 12-105.1, 12-111.1).

Separately, damaging or climbing public trees with spurs is an offense (Sec. 9-51), and attaching newsracks to trees is prohibited (Sec. 12-70). For specific fee amounts and replacement size standards, the ordinance refers to schedules set by the senior urban forestry and landscape planner rather than stating them here.
