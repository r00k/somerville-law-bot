Somerville regulates fences primarily through the Zoning Ordinance, Section 10.5.1. In general, fences may be placed up to and along any lot line (Sec. 10.5.1). However, if you install a fence directly on a shared side or rear lot line, you need the consent of the abutting property owner. Fences do not require a Building Permit, and are erected at the property owner's own risk.

Orientation matters: the fence posts and supporting rails must face inward toward your own property, and the finished (smooth) face must be oriented toward the neighbor or the street (Sec. 10.5.1, with Figure 10.5.1 illustrating this).

Height and opacity limits depend on where the fence sits:
- In the **frontage area** (the front of the lot, including both frontages on a corner lot), fences may be up to 4 feet tall and no more than 50% opaque (Sec. 10.5.1).
- **Behind the frontage area** (internal to the lot), fences may be up to 6 feet tall, unless screening is required under Section 10.7 (Screening).

Architectural fence and gate posts integrated into the design may go slightly higher — up to 4.5 feet in the frontage area and 6.5 feet behind it (Sec. 10.5.1).

Restrictions: fences are prohibited within any existing or required drainage or utility easement, and barbed wire and concertina wire are banned entirely (Sec. 10.5.1).

One exception to the height rule: through a Special Permit or Site Plan Approval, the Review Boards may require a privacy fence behind the frontage area to be up to 8 feet tall to address neighbors' privacy concerns.

Note: these sections don't address common questions like fence maintenance, cost-sharing with neighbors, or disputes over property lines — consult a professional for those. For screening requirements, see the related Screening topic (Section 10.7).
