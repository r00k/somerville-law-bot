Somerville's zoning treats a home business as a "Home Occupation," which is an **accessory use** category — meaning it's a secondary activity carried out alongside living in a home. The glossary (zon:162) defines Home Occupation and directs you to § 9.2.14.b for the actual rules. Within that category, the ordinance recognizes several specific types:

- **Home-Based Business** (zon:158, § 9.2.14.b.iv)
- **Home-Based Child Day Care** (zon:160, § 9.2.14.b.v)
- **Home Office** (zon:161, § 9.2.14.b.vi)
- **Home-Based Business Vehicle Parking** as an accessory parking use (zon:159, § 9.2.14.d.i)

An important caveat: the sections provided here are only glossary/definition entries. They tell you these use types exist and where to find them, but **the actual operating rules** — such as square-footage limits, number of employees, signage, customer traffic, permitted districts, or whether a permit is required — live in § 9.2.14 itself, which was **not included in the text provided here**. To answer practical questions about what you're allowed to do, you'll need to consult the full text of § 9.2.14.b.

Note also that a **Work/Live Creative Studio** (zon:325) is a different thing — it's a *principal* use under Arts & Creative Enterprise (§ 9.2.2.g Artisanal Production), not a home occupation, so it follows separate rules.

The Home-Based Child Day Care definition was added by Ord. 2024-13 (11/26/2024).
