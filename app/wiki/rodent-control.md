Somerville requires all property owners and occupants to keep their premises free of rats, mice, and other rodents, with the goal of limiting rodent populations and preventing disease (Sec. 11-38(a), (c)). This rule sits in the trash/public works chapter, so it's closely tied to garbage and trash storage requirements.

Key duties under Sec. 11-38(c) include rodent-proofing to keep rodents away from food and water sources (including pet food and bird food) and preventing the buildup of anything that feeds or shelters rodents — garbage, litter, lumber, firewood, tree limbs, junked vehicles, construction debris, overgrown vegetation, and animal waste, among others.

If there's an actual infestation — shown by burrows, droppings, gnaw marks, or tracks (the definition in Sec. 11-38(b)) — the owner or occupant must eradicate it at their own expense and do whatever the superintendent of inspectional services or board of health reasonably requires, which can include extermination by a state-licensed exterminator (Sec. 11-38(d)(1)). Note that only a Massachusetts-licensed pest control company qualifies as an "exterminator" here.

Demolition, construction, land-clearing, and subsurface right-of-way projects can trigger mandatory extermination programs beginning at least seven days before work starts and continuing until substantial completion, unless waived (Sec. 11-38(d)(2)).

Enforcement: inspectors may enter property at reasonable times to inspect (with a warrant if entry is refused) (Sec. 11-38(f)). Violations bring a written correction order to be fixed within a time set by officials, not to exceed 21 days (Sec. 11-38(e)). Fines follow the noncriminal disposition process and Sec. 1-11, with each day a separate offense; you can request a hearing before a municipal hearing officer to contest a fine (Sec. 11-38(i)). If you don't comply, the city can do the work itself and place a lien on your property for the cost (Sec. 11-38(j)).

One area gotcha: if the board of health believes a whole geographic area is infested, it can hold a public hearing (14 days' notice to landowners in the area and within 300 feet) and then require everyone in that area to bait their property or take other measures (Sec. 11-38(g)).

The sections don't specify dollar amounts for fines or list approved baiting methods; check Sec. 1-11 and any regulations the superintendent adopts under Sec. 11-38(h) for those details.
