Somerville regulates household trash, recycling, and related waste mostly under Chapter 11, Article II. For homes of six units or less, put refuse out no earlier than 4:00 p.m. the day before collection and no later than 7:00 a.m. on collection day, and remove empty cans and lids by 7:00 p.m. that day (Sec. 11-31(g)). There is one collection day per week; if you miss the truck, the trash is your responsibility. Use approved covered barrels — no single container may exceed 50 pounds or 64 gallons, and cardboard boxes/barrels count as unapproved bulk items (Sec. 11-31(h)).

Recycling is mandatory for every building, whether or not the city collects it (Sec. 11-40). Recyclables must be separated from trash, placed only in authorized recycling receptacles (not plastic garbage bags), and the city may refuse to pick up your rubbish if recyclables aren't separated (Sec. 11-40(d)). The specific list of accepted materials is set by the DPW commissioner's regulations, not spelled out in the ordinance itself. Once placed at the curb, recyclables become city property, and it's a violation for others to take them (Sec. 11-40(d)).

Yard waste must be cut to lengths under three feet and tied in bundles, or placed in paper leaf bags or barrels with DPW "yard waste" stickers (Sec. 11-31(j)). Hazardous waste (gasoline, batteries, tires, etc.) must not be set out (Sec. 11-31(k)). Bulk items like appliances require doors removed and a purchased white goods disposal sticker (Sec. 11-31(l)); owner-done building-material repairs have their own limits (Sec. 11-31(m)).

Gotchas: dwellings of seven or more units pay $150/unit annually for city collection (Sec. 11-35); commercial trash follows separate rules and fees (Sec. 11-34, Sec. 8-16); dumpsters require a Board of Health license and follow Appendix D rules. It's illegal to dump refuse on others' property (Sec. 11-31(o)) or to disturb/pick trash set out for collection (Sec. 11-32, Sec. 11-41). Violations are fined under Sec. 1-11, often per day.

The provided sections do not address residential curbside **composting** or organics collection programs — the corpus covers trash, recycling, and yard waste but not a dedicated compost service. Related topics: littering and dumping on public property (Sec. 12-3), sidewalk trash removal (Sec. 11-43, Sec. 11-44), and rodent control (Sec. 11-38).
