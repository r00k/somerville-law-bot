Somerville's ordinances mostly cover *who* collects taxes and fees and the administrative rules around them, rather than setting specific property tax or excise rates — the property tax rate itself is not stated in these sections. The Board of Assessors handles assessments (Div. 2, and Charter § 36), and the city treasurer/collector of taxes collects and receives debts due the city (Sec. 2-151). If an account stays unpaid for three months, the treasurer may pursue legal collection, or sooner if the city's interests require (Sec. 2-151). The collector must annually publish a list, kept for public reference, of real estate sold for nonpayment of taxes and assessments (Sec. 2-157).

**Brownfields abatements (Sec. 2-158):** For sites zoned commercial or industrial, the tax collector may negotiate an agreement to abate outstanding interest, penalties, and real estate tax on contaminated sites being cleaned up. The agreement must be approved by the chief financial officer and city solicitor and signed/notarized by the owner.

**Taxes as a condition of licenses (Sec. 8-3):** This is a common gotcha — the city can deny, revoke, or suspend most local licenses and permits (including renewals and building permits) if you owe local taxes, fees, assessments, betterments, or other charges for 12 months or more. You get written notice and a hearing (at least 14 days out), and you can enter a payment agreement or file a good-faith abatement application to avoid denial. Certain licenses are exempt (e.g., dog, marriage, hunting/fishing licenses). The city council may waive denial in limited cases.

**Water/sewer charges (Sec. 11-125):** Rates are set by the water/sewer director with mayor and council approval after a public hearing; bills are due within 45 days, with interest after that. Residents who own and occupy their home and qualify for certain elderly/other exemptions get a 25% discount on water/sewer bills (subsection (f)). Appeals of contested bills go to the commissioner.

For questions about the actual tax rate, exemption amounts, or excise tax procedures, these sections don't provide answers — those are largely governed by Massachusetts General Laws referenced throughout.

*This summary is for general orientation only. The cited section text above is authoritative — always verify anything that matters against the linked sections, not this summary.*
