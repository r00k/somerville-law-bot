Somerville regulates both personal marijuana use in public and the licensing of cannabis businesses.

**Public use and sales.** Under Sec. 9-3, you may not give, sell, deliver, or consume marijuana (or THC) in public parks, playgrounds, rest areas, school grounds, recreational areas, swimming pools, skating rinks, or the common areas of city housing authority projects — and you may not consume within 50 feet of those places. "Common areas" is defined broadly to include hallways, stairwells, front steps, basements, rooftops, sidewalks, parking areas, roadways, and park areas (Sec. 9-3(a)). No one may sell or deliver marijuana unless licensed by both the city Licensing Commission and the state Cannabis Control Commission (CCC) and permitted under the zoning ordinance (Sec. 9-3(b)). Violations can lead to confiscation and a fine under Sec. 1-11 (Sec. 9-3(c)).

**Business licensing.** Sec. 2-221 governs adult-use marijuana establishments — cultivators, product manufacturers, retailers, testing labs, research facilities, transporters, microbusinesses, and craft cooperatives (defined in Sec. 2-221(a)). Almost all types need a local license from the Licensing Commission; only transporters are exempt (Sec. 2-221(b)(1)). Retail licenses must number at least 20% of the city's package-store liquor licenses, but a temporary cap of 12 retail establishments applies (expiring after 24 months unless amended) (Sec. 2-221(b)(1)).

**Equity priority.** For five years, the Commission generally issues licenses only to "priority applicants" — including state-certified economic empowerment applicants, majority Somerville-resident-owned entities, cooperatives, and existing medical dispensaries (Sec. 2-221(b)(2)). Group B applicants can only be licensed when Group A holdings are equal or greater.

**Key conditions/gotchas.** Retail stores must be at least 300 feet from primary/secondary schools and cannot exceed 10,000 sq. ft. (zoning § 9.2.4.d); a special permit within 500 feet of a school requires special findings. Licenses require a Community Host Agreement, expire after five years, are non-transferable, and can be revoked for cause (Sec. 2-221(g)–(k)). Retailers may **not** deliver to consumers or allow on-site social consumption (Sec. 2-221(a), definition of marijuana retailer).

The provided sections do not address home cultivation or personal possession limits for individuals — those are governed by state law, not these ordinances. See also the zoning use categories in § 9.2.4 for where each cannabis business type may locate.
