Building permits are handled by the Building Official / Inspectional Services Department (ISD). Under the zoning ordinance, no development of any lot may proceed until a Building Permit is issued (Sec. 15.3.5), and the permit certifies that construction documents comply with the Massachusetts State Building Code (780 CMR) and City ordinances. The Building Official must issue the permit or explain the refusal in writing within 30 days of a completed application (Sec. 15.3.5). Under the Code of Ordinances, the superintendent of buildings grants permits for new buildings, additions, and alterations after confirming compliance with the building code (Sec. 11-101), and also issues occupancy permits and change-of-use permits.

Common gotchas:
- **Timing/expiration:** Construction must begin within 6 months of permit issuance and proceed continuously, or the permit lapses (Sec. 15.3.5).
- **Zoning approvals come first:** If your project needs a Special Permit, Site Plan Approval, or Hardship Variance, the Building Official cannot issue the Building Permit until that approval is granted (Secs. 15.2.1, 15.3.2, 15.2.3).
- **Certificate of Occupancy:** You can't occupy or use the property, or change its use, until a Certificate of Occupancy is issued after final inspection (Sec. 15.4.1).
- **Site safety plan:** For covered construction, alteration, or demolition, ISD won't issue a permit until an approved site safety plan is submitted, plus a designated site safety coordinator with OSHA 10-hour certification (Secs. 8-379, 8-380). Projects with a homeowner licensure exemption under 780 CMR are excepted (Sec. 8-377).
- **Inspections:** Applying consents to ISD entering the property for inspections (Sec. 15.3.5), and no building may be lathed until the inspector approves (Sec. 11-103).

**Appeals:** A denial or Building Official decision can be challenged through an Administrative Appeal to the Zoning Board of Appeals (Sec. 15.5.2) or, for State Building Code disputes, a Building Code Appeal to the state board (Sec. 15.5.1).

**Related topics:** Demolition permits carry extra abutter-notification and fire-detail rules (Sec. 11-105); driveways and paving that don't require a building permit have their own permit process (Sec. 11-88); moving or enlarging structures in streets requires City Council permission (Secs. 11-108, 11-110). For specific fee amounts and application forms, this text doesn't list them — check with ISD, as the Building Official sets forms and fees (Sec. 15.7.1).
