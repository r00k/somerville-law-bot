Somerville regulates where dogs can go, how they must be controlled, nuisance behavior, and what happens when dogs are picked up.

**Leash / at-large rules.** Under Sec. 3-34, it is unlawful to let your dog run loose anywhere in the city. A dog is considered "at-large" unless it is on the owner's own property, in a properly posted off-leash area (following the posted rules), controlled by a chain or leash no longer than six feet, or inside a vehicle or boat. Sec. 3-4 additionally bars dogs from swimming in park ponds or damaging flower beds, shrubs, or other city property in parks.

**Parks, off-leash areas, and dog parks.** Under Sec. 12-51, the commissioner of public works may post areas "No Dogs Allowed," and no one may bring a dog (leashed or not) into a posted area. The commissioner may also designate enclosed off-leash areas and post rules at every entrance; those rules take effect within 45 days of submission to the city council unless the council says otherwise. Note: dogs under a restraint/muzzle order (Sec. 3-32) or identified under Sec. 3-33 may not use off-leash areas.

**Nuisance, barking, and biting.** Sec. 3-33 prohibits owning a dog that disturbs others by barking, biting, howling, or harassing. Residents can file a written complaint with the police under Sec. 3-31; police may investigate and order a dog restrained or muzzled. Sec. 3-32 lists specific grounds for ordering restraint or muzzling, including biting a person, damaging property, or chasing vehicles. Sec. 3-37 covers dogs in heat that attract other dogs and cause disturbances.

**Impoundment.** Under Sec. 3-35, a loose dog is confined; the owner has ten days to claim it and pay charges before it may be sold or destroyed. Sec. 3-36 sets confinement charges at $15.00 per day.

**Penalties.** Sec. 3-38 sets fines (referencing Sec. 1-11) for violations, including failure to curb your dog, and allows revocation of an animal permit or license for repeat or serious violations. Each day can count as a separate offense.

**Restaurants.** Sec. 6-24 lets the board of health set rules allowing dogs in restaurant outdoor seating areas.

**Gotchas / not covered here:** These sections do not spell out dog licensing requirements or fees, vaccination rules, or the specific dollar amounts of fines (see Sec. 1-11 for penalty amounts). For breeding/keeping multiple dogs at home, see the zoning "Hobby Kennel" use (§ 9.2.14.b.iii).
