Somerville regulates several categories of single-use plastics and foam, mostly under Chapter 9, Article VI.

**Expanded polystyrene ("styrofoam") — Sec. 9-100 through 9-104.** Food establishments may not serve prepared food in disposable containers made from expanded polystyrene, and city departments and city contractors/vendors may not use such containers in official duties or in a city facility (Sec. 9-102). "Disposable food service container" covers plates, cups, bowls, trays, lidded containers, straws, cup lids, and utensils, but not packaging for unprepared foods (Sec. 9-101). City employees may still buy or use these items for personal use (Sec. 9-102(b)). The city maintains a list of compostable/recyclable alternatives (Sec. 9-103). Enforcement is by the health and inspectional services departments, with fines under Sec. 1-11 and possible permit suspension (Sec. 9-104).

**Plastic shopping bag ban — Sec. 9-105 through 9-105.4.** No retail establishment may give customers a disposable plastic shopping (checkout) bag (Sec. 9-105.2). "Retail establishment" is broad — restaurants, pharmacies, grocery and liquor stores, and more (Sec. 9-105.1). Note a common gotcha: even compostable/biodegradable plastic bags are prohibited, but reusable bags (durable, 125+ uses), produce/product bags, laundry/dry-cleaner bags, newspaper bags, and bags for frozen food/meat/fish are exempt (Sec. 9-105.1). Stores may still sell reusable bags or offer recyclable paper bags. First offense gets a warning with at least 14 days to fix; later fines follow Sec. 1-11(b) (Sec. 9-105.3).

**Plastic straws, stirrers, and cutlery — Sec. 9-106 through 9-106.4.** Food establishments may only dispense plastic straws, stirrers, or cutlery on customer request (Sec. 9-106.2), and must post a sign when straws are available on request. Exemptions cover medical necessity, pre-packaged items, health care facilities, and declared emergencies (Sec. 9-106.2(b)). Retail stores may still sell plastic cutlery, and individuals may use their own.

**Child-safety rule for plastic bags — Sec. 8-11.** Separately, any plastic bag sold in retail trade must be perforated, have safety openings, or be labeled "Dangerous to Children," and discarded bags accessible to children must be torn open (Sec. 8-11). An older at-store recycling program (Sec. 9-106.5–9-106.7) also required stores of 5,000+ sq. ft. to provide bag recycling bins.
