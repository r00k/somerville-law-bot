Somerville's ordinances set operating hours in three different contexts: city offices, private businesses, and public parks.

**City offices** (Sec. 2-4): City Hall is open Monday–Wednesday 8:30 a.m.–4:30 p.m., Thursday 8:30 a.m.–7:30 p.m., and Friday 8:30 a.m.–12:30 p.m., with legal holidays excepted. Other city departments outside City Hall are generally open Monday–Friday, 8:30 a.m.–4:30 p.m. (except School Committee and public library, which set their own). These hours can change if agreed by the mayor and collective bargaining units and approved by the city council.

**Retail, restaurant and service businesses** (Sec. 8-7): No establishment open to the public may conduct regular business between midnight and 5:00 a.m. unless the city council grants an extended hours permit (Sec. 8-7(a), (d)). "Restaurant" here does *not* include places licensed to serve or sell alcohol. Businesses must post their hours on the main window or door (Sec. 8-7(c)), and any extended-hours permit must be posted in public view. Permits expire annually on May 15 (Sec. 8-7(e)), and the council may require a public hearing—with certified-mail notice to property owners within 300 feet and on-site posting—when neighborhood impacts are a concern (Sec. 8-7(f)). Gotchas: each day open in violation counts as a separate offense (Sec. 8-7(i), (k), penalties per Sec. 1-11), and a business told it's violating its approved hours must immediately stop after midnight (Sec. 8-7(g)). Permits can't be revoked or modified without notice and a hearing (Sec. 8-7(h)).

**Parks and playgrounds** (Sec. 12-48): Closed to the public daily from 10:00 p.m. to 8:00 a.m., except city personnel on city business. Groups wanting to use park facilities during those closed hours (e.g., athletic events) must first get a permit from the city council (Sec. 12-49); people viewing or participating in duly-organized events that run past 10:00 p.m. are exempt.

For related licensing rules, see Chapter 8 (Licenses, Permits and Business Regulations). The sections here do not address hours for alcohol-serving establishments, which are handled under separate liquor licensing rules not included above.
