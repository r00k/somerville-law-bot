Somerville's City Council is the city's legislative body. Under Section 2-1 it has 11 members serving 2-year terms: four councilors at-large (elected citywide) and seven ward councilors (one from each ward). Any voter can run for at-large; ward candidates must live in the ward they seek (Section 2-1(c)). The council holds all legislative power of the city (Section 2-4) and picks a president and vice president each year (Section 2-6).

Meetings are governed by the Rules of the City Council (Appendix B). Regular meetings are set by ordinance; special meetings can be called by the mayor, the president, or six members (Rule 1, Section 2-5(e)). A majority of members is a quorum (Rule 5, Section 2-5(b)), and all sessions are open to the public (Section 2-5(f)). Meetings are video- or audio-recorded and posted online when equipment is available (Rule 8). The order of business is fixed by Rule 32, and the city clerk sets deadlines for agenda items — generally four workdays ahead for non-members and three for members (Rule 4).

To get an item before the council, residents usually work through a councilor who can sponsor a member of the public to speak (Rule 20), or file a group petition signed by at least 50 voters, which triggers a required public hearing and council action within set timeframes (Section 2-11). Petitions needing hearings get notice arranged by the clerk, with the petitioner paying notice costs (Secs. 2-44, 2-45, 2-46).

A key "gotcha" is the charter objection (Section 2-9(b), Rule 54): a single member can delay a vote to the next meeting, and two or more push it to the next regular meeting — but only once per matter. Ordinances go to the mayor, who can veto; the council overrides with 8 votes (Rule 55, Section 2-9). The council also confirms many mayoral appointments (Section 2-8) and reviews the budget, including a mid-year fiscal report each February (Sec. 2-48) and a joint session with the school committee (Sec. 2-47).

Councilor pay is set by ordinance and cannot change mid-year or between an election and the new council's qualification (Secs. 2-41, 2-42, Section 2-3). For how vacant seats are filled, see Section 2-12. This page does not cover election procedures in detail; see related election topics.
