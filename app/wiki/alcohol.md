Somerville regulates both who may sell alcohol and where and how it may be consumed or advertised. Most retail and on-premises sales require a license from the Somerville Licensing Commission under state law (M.G.L. c. 138). The city charter appendix (Article IX) sets the number of licenses available: the commission may issue up to 20 all-alcohol and 9 wine-and-malt licenses under c. 138 § 15 (Sec. 2), plus various batches of additional on-premises licenses tied to specific areas — for example, up to 27 in the Assembly Square district (Sec. 3) and blocks of licenses restricted to "Transformative Areas" (Inner Belt, Brickbottom, Boynton Yards) and "Enhancement Areas" (Union Square, Davis Square, Ball Square, etc.) (Secs. 4 and 6). A gotcha: many of these newer, area-restricted licenses cannot be sold or transferred; if a business closes or fails to renew, the license reverts physically to the commission for reissue to a new applicant at the same or similarly located premises (Secs. 4–7).

Getting or expanding a license requires public notice in a local newspaper and a public hearing held at least ten days later (Sec. 2-219). The commission cannot grant a liquor license unless the premises comply with the state building code and city zoning; a written report from the building superintendent is required, and any existing violation blocks the license (Sec. 2-220). Zoning classifies "Alcohol Sales" and "Bar, Restaurant, or Tavern" as specific use types (§ 9.2.12.b.ii and § 9.2.7.a).

For public consumption, Sec. 9-1 bans possessing (open or unopened) or drinking alcohol in parks, playgrounds, school grounds, recreation areas, pools, and rinks, and bans open containers on streets — with exceptions for permitted public events, licensed outdoor café seating (see Sec. 12-14), and permitted festivals (Sec. 8-14). Selling any alcoholic beverage to anyone under 21 is prohibited (Sec. 9-2). Licensed establishments may not permit inhaling vaporized alcohol (Sec. 9-95).

Outdoor alcohol advertising is restricted: no alcohol billboards or signs within 500 feet of playgrounds, schools, places of worship, or childcare facilities, and none on city-owned property, though businesses may apply for an exemption (Sec. 12-23); enforcement is by Inspectional Services (Sec. 12-25).

The provided sections do not spell out application fees, hours of operation, or day-to-day operating rules beyond the fee-parity note (Sec. 8) — contact the Licensing Commission for those specifics.
