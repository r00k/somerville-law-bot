Somerville's zoning treats detached accessory dwellings mainly through the **Backyard Cottage** building type (Sec. 3.1.12), allowed in the Neighborhood Residence (NR) district. A Backyard Cottage is a small, detached accessory building — the ordinance describes it as space for one small dwelling unit, a home occupation, a playhouse, or parking — placed on the same lot as a principal building. This is Somerville's closest equivalent to what residents call an "in-law apartment," "granny flat," or "ADU."

Key rules from Sec. 3.1.12:
- **Size and height:** Maximum floor plate of 576 sq ft, main massing width up to 24 ft and depth up to 32 ft, story height up to 12 ft, and a maximum of 1.5 stories. Roof must be flat or gable.
- **Units:** No more than one (1) dwelling unit.
- **Setbacks:** Minimum 3 ft side and rear setbacks (with an alley, rear can be 3 or 20 ft), and a minimum 10 ft separation from the principal building.
- **Lot coverage and Green Score:** Must match the principal building's limits.
- **Design/safety:** A 3-ft paved walkway wide enough for an ambulance cot is required, utilities must be buried underground, and an automatic sprinkler system is required for residential cottages unless the lot is a corner lot, has a driveway leading to the cottage, or abuts an alley.

The general terms "Accessory Building" (Sec. 2.1) and "Accessory Use" (Sec. 2.1) confirm these structures must be incidental to a principal use on the same lot.

**Gaps to note:** The provided sections do not address interior in-law units built inside an existing house, third-story additions, or general home additions/expansions — nor do they spell out the permit or approval process (by-right vs. special permit) for a Backyard Cottage. For those questions, check the district use tables and permitting sections of the zoning ordinance, which are not included here.
