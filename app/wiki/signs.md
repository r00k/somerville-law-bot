Somerville regulates signs both through its Zoning Ordinance (commercial signs, Sec. 10.8) and through street/sidewalk and public-property ordinances. If you own a business, the zoning rules in Sec. 10.8 govern your storefront signage.

**Permitted commercial sign types (Sec. 10.8.3):** Only certain types are allowed "by right" — blade, wall, awning/canopy, suspended, sidewalk, window, display case, vertical blade/banner, yard, wall mural, and roof/skyline signs. Types not listed — including billboards, monument signs, and pylon signs — are prohibited. Each sign type has its own size, height, projection, and location limits (Secs. 10.8.10–10.8.20). For example, a wall sign maxes out at 40 sq. ft. and is only for ground-story businesses (Sec. 10.8.11); a blade sign maxes at 6 sq. ft./side (Sec. 10.8.10); a window sign can cover up to 20% of window glass (Sec. 10.8.15).

**Exempt signs (Sec. 10.8.4):** Government/public-notice signs, engraved building names, small credit-card/trade signs, clocks, flags, historic building IDs, holiday and yard-sale signs, political yard signs, and decorative art are not regulated as commercial signs.

**Prohibited features (Sec. 10.8.5):** Flashing/moving/electronic-copy signs, signs that make noise or emit odor, signs on trees or fences, billboards, inflatable signs, and signs on parked vehicles used as advertising.

**Temporary signs (Sec. 10.8.7):** Allowed up to 30 total days; banners, construction signs, and real-estate signs each have specific size and timing limits.

**Maintenance & removal:** Signs must be kept in good condition, with damage repaired within 30 days of notice (Sec. 10.8.6), and removed within 30 days after a business vacates (Sec. 10.8.8). Illumination rules are in Sec. 10.8.9. Nonconforming (older, noncompliant) signs may change content but can't grow larger, and you can't add new signs without bringing old ones into compliance (Sec. 14.1.8).

**Signs over/on streets and sidewalks:** Placing signs, awnings, or structures on or over a public way requires a city council permit (Sec. 12-14). Signs over public ways can't exceed six feet in length or projection or hang lower than nine feet above the sidewalk (Sec. 12-15); awnings must be at least seven feet high (Sec. 12-16). The DPW highway superintendent can remove violating signs (Sec. 12-19).

**Posting on public/private property:** You may not attach signs to trees, poles, or structures in parks or public ways, or post on city property, without the mayor's consent (Sec. 9-48), and you may not post on private property without the owner's consent (Sec. 9-49). Tampering with posted notices is prohibited (Sec. 9-50).

**Alcohol advertising:** Outdoor alcohol ads are banned within 500 feet of playgrounds, schools, places of worship, and childcare facilities, with a possible exemption on application (Secs. 12-21 through 12-25).

One gotcha: the zoning section notes it is not meant to regulate sign *content* in ways barred by the constitution (Sec. 10.8.2). The provided sections do not spell out the exact permit application process or fees for a business sign — check with the city's building/inspectional services for that.

*This summary is for general orientation only. The cited section text above is authoritative — always verify anything that matters against the linked sections, not this summary.*
