Somerville's animal rules are split across the Code of Ordinances Chapter 3 (Animals), Board of Health regulations (Appendix D), and the Zoning Ordinance.

**Backyard chickens/hens.** You must get a permit from the Board of Health before keeping hens (Appendix D, Board of Health Regulations for the Keeping of Hens). The fee is $50 for the first year and $25 for annual renewal. Key rules: a maximum of **six (6) hens per lot**, and **roosters are banned in Somerville** entirely. A "hen" means a female chicken; a henhouse is the enclosed structure and a pen is the enclosed outdoor space. You must notify abutters (neighbors) when applying, watch the Board of Health video, provide at least 2 sq ft of henhouse and 4 sq ft of pen per hen, keep hens confined to your property and out of the living area of your home, store feed in rodent-proof containers, clean the henhouse weekly, and manage waste and odors so nothing is perceptible at property boundaries. Noise must comply with the City's Noise Ordinance. Larger structures (over 120 sq ft or 10 ft tall, or with electrical/plumbing) require building permits. The Board can suspend, revoke, or refuse to renew a permit, and can order hens removed at the owner's expense if they cause disturbance or damage. Existing henhouses at the time the ordinance passed are grandfathered for hens over the six-hen limit until those hens expire.

**Livestock and other animals.** No domestic fowl, swine, goat, sheep, ox, cow, horse, or other grazing animal may be allowed to run at-large or graze on city streets, parks, or public grounds (Sec. 3-4). You may not tie a horse or other animal to a street tree (Sec. 3-1) or frighten horses (Sec. 3-2). Feeding or baiting animals in streets requires a permit, and feeding birds on sidewalks, streets, parks, or public property is prohibited (Sec. 3-3).

**Non-domesticated animals.** Displaying living non-domesticated animals for entertainment (circuses, carnivals, animal acts, rides, races) is banned on both public and private property (Sec. 3-39), with exceptions for domestic/farm animals (except competitive races) and MSPCA-approved educational exhibits.

**Dogs** are covered by their own rules (Secs. 3-31 through 3-38, plus Secs. 12-51 and 6-24) — see a related dogs topic for leash, at-large, nuisance, and off-leash area details.

**Gotchas:** The corpus doesn't spell out zoning limits on where hens/aviculture are permitted beyond referencing the Urban Agriculture use category (Zoning §§ 9.2.13, 9.2.14) — check zoning for your district. Selling farm-fresh eggs must comply with state storage rules (eggs kept at 45°F).
