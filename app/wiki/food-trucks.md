Somerville regulates anyone who stores, prepares, sells, or serves food or beverages from a truck or cart — including ice cream and non-ice cream vendors — under Chapter 8, Article XIII (Sec. 8-361 defines "mobile food vendor").

**License required.** No person or business, including religious or charitable groups, may operate as a mobile food vendor in any public, private, or restricted space without a license from the Health Division of Inspectional Services, and the license must be posted where customers can see it (Sec. 8-362). Operations with a temporary event permit from the Board of Health are exempt. Also, this article does not apply to a food truck vendor who was lawfully operating under a hawker-and-peddler license when the ordinance was enacted (Sec. 8-370).

**Applying and renewing.** Apply on the City Clerk's form; fees are set under state law G.L. c. 40, s. 22F (Sec. 8-363). You must hold a valid state hawker's license and any applicable Board of Health license — if either is revoked or lapses, your mobile food vendor license automatically becomes void, and you must notify the City Clerk within ten days (Sec. 8-363). Licenses renew annually with a fee, but renewal does not guarantee your prior route or location (Sec. 8-364). Multiple departments weigh factors like menu, hours, location, noise, traffic, parking, neighborhood character, and impact on existing restaurants when deciding (Sec. 8-365).

**Operating rules (Sec. 8-366).** A license does not give you exclusive rights to any route or spot. No Styrofoam is allowed; vendors must offer a public waste container (emptied at their own expense) and clean litter within 100 feet. You cannot operate outside your approved hours, and nothing (tables, chairs, signs, coolers) may be placed on the public sidewalk without Council approval. You may not park within 50 feet of a restaurant entrance without that restaurant owner's written consent. Vendors are encouraged (not required) to use compostable articles and offer at least 25% healthy meal options.

**Transfers, fines, and appeals.** Licenses are personal and cannot be sold, lent, leased, or transferred for value; unauthorized transfers void the license and carry a fine (Sec. 8-367). Violations can draw daily fines and the license can be suspended, revoked, or modified for cause after notice and a hearing (Sec. 8-368). Appeals of violations go to the City Hearing Officer (Sec. 8-371). The City may impose further conditions on any license (Sec. 8-369).

Note: the exact fee amounts and specific approved locations/routes are not listed in these sections — check with the Health Division and City Clerk. See also Board of Health regulations, traffic and parking rules, and the noise ordinance, which vendors must also follow (Sec. 8-366(d)).
