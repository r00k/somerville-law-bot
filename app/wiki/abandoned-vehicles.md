Somerville regulates junk, unregistered, and abandoned vehicles mainly through Chapter 9 of the Code of Ordinances.

**Abandoning a vehicle.** It is illegal to abandon or discard a motor vehicle (or vehicle parts) on any public or private property in the city (Sec. 9-91), punishable under the general penalty in Sec. 1-11. The Commissioner of Public Works (or their designee) may remove any abandoned or discarded vehicle or part, and the person who abandoned it is liable to the city for removal costs (Sec. 9-92). State law (M.G.L. c. 90, §§ 22B–22C) also applies.

**Unregistered vehicles on private property.** You may not store an unregistered motor vehicle on private property for more than 90 days, and no more than one unregistered vehicle at a time (Sec. 9-57(b)). This does not apply to vehicles kept inside an enclosed building or to businesses licensed under G.L. c. 140, § 59.

**Inoperable vehicles.** You cannot store an "inoperable" vehicle on private property—one that has been unable to move under its own power for at least seven days, or has had engine, wheels, windshield, or other parts removed or damaged (Sec. 9-57(c)). Exceptions again cover vehicles kept in a building and licensed businesses.

**Repairs and outdoor storage.** Repairing or maintaining a vehicle on any street or public property is prohibited (Sec. 9-57(a)). One recreational vehicle, boat, or utility trailer may be stored outdoors on a lot with a dwelling, but it must sit behind the front facade, be screened by solid fencing at least six feet high, and stay in good repair (Sec. 9-57(d)).

**Unregistered/unlicensed vehicles on streets.** Operating an unlicensed motorized vehicle (mini-motorcycles, ATVs, motorized skateboards) on a public way or city sidewalk carries a $50 fine per offense; Segways and disability mobility devices are exempt (Sec. 9-94).

**Penalties.** Under Sec. 9-57(e), each day of a violation is a separate offense. For towing tied to snow removal, see Sec. 11-2. The corpus does not specify how to report a suspected abandoned vehicle or a timeline for city removal.
