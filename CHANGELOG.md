# Somerville Law Changelog

Substantive changes to Somerville municipal law observed between refreshes of this corpus. Each entry compares a new snapshot against the previous one; editorial or formatting-only differences are omitted. This is a convenience summary, not an official record — see the [amendment history in the official publication](https://online.encodeplus.com/regs/somerville-ma/doc-viewer.aspx) for authoritative details.

## 2026-07-09 (previous snapshot: 2026-03-07)

### Code of Ordinances

**Sec. 2-6 — Somerville Welcoming Community Ordinance** substantially amended:

- **New: role of city staff in law enforcement action** (subsection (e)(3)). City employees may not assist, cooperate with, or allow city resources to be used to facilitate federal or out-of-state operations that target First Amendment–protected activity or that seek to identify, locate, arrest, or penalize a person for purposes of civil federal immigration enforcement, except as required by federal or state law.
- **Raids and other immigration enforcement actions** (now (e)(11)): the prior carve-out allowing Somerville Police to provide support services during federal operations (traffic perimeters, traffic control, police escorts) was removed. Instead, officers dispatched to the scene of a federal immigration enforcement action must protect the safety of all on scene, take reasonable steps to verify the identity of the federal agents, and document the activity and any identification provided.
- **New: contract review** ((e)(13)). Before awarding or renewing agreements for goods or services, city agencies must obtain a Law Department opinion on compliance; agreements with federal or out-of-state agencies that facilitate information sharing or surveillance for civil immigration enforcement (and related purposes) are prohibited, and non-compliant agreements must be amended or terminated.
- **New: enforcement subsection** ((g)). The ordinance is enforced by the mayor's office or designee; a violation constitutes an injury, and injured persons may sue the city for injunctive relief, declaratory relief, or a writ of mandamus. City employees who report violations receive whistleblower protections under M.G.L. c. 149, § 185. Subsequent subsections re-lettered ((g) Reporting → (h), (h) Compliance with federal law → (i), (i) Other → (j)).
- **Federal requests for information** ((e)(6)): the public-records exception is now limited to requests made "in the course of enforcing federal civil immigration."
- **Compliance with federal law** (now (i)): information sharing consistent with 8 U.S.C. § 1373 now also permitted "when compelled by judicial order."
- The severability clause formerly at the end of subsection (i) *Other* was removed; a narrower savings clause appears in the new enforcement subsection ((g)(4)).
- Various mandatory-language tightening throughout (e.g., "will treat" → "shall treat", "will not initiate" → "may not initiate").

### Zoning Ordinance

**Ord. 2026-03 (adopted 05/14/2026) — Affordable Housing overlay, Sections 8.1.2 and 8.1.6:**

- **8.1.2 Purpose**: wording fix only ("incentive" → "incentivize").
- **8.1.6 Mid-Rise Districts**: Table 8.1.6, retitled "Superseding Dimensional Standards," now **supersedes all** lot, building-placement, massing & height, and uses & features standards of the underlying district for Apartment and General Buildings (previously it "superseded or supplemented" specified standards), and the 5% dimensional-deviation allowance now references the table directly. Standards changes for both building types:
  - **Height increased**: max stories for MR3–MR6 lots not abutting Neighborhood Residence raised from 7 to 8; max building height raised from 86 ft to 96 ft (Apartment Building) and 88 ft to 100 ft (General Building).
  - **Top-story step-back requirement removed** (formerly 10 ft for buildings of 5+ stories).
  - **New lot-development standards** enumerated in the table: lot coverage max 100%, green score 0.20 min / 0.25 ideal, open space min 10%, curb setback min 12 ft, front setbacks 0–15 ft.
  - **Side and rear setbacks restructured** by abutting condition (alley/rail right-of-way, non-NR/LHD, Neighborhood Residence or Local Historic District), with the story-based NR/LHD rows extended from 7 to 8 stories, and the ≥100 ft side-setback trigger corrected from lot depth to lot width.
  - **New façade and occupancy standards**: fenestration 15% min / 50% max; General Buildings require at least one ground-story commercial unit.
